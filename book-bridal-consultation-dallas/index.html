What to Bring to Your Consultation

👗

Your Gown

Bring the gown you’d like worked on. In its bag if you have one. The consultation can’t happen without seeing the actual gown. We assess fabric, fit, embellishments, and condition in person.

👠

Your Wedding Shoes

Hemming can’t be accurately pinned without your exact wedding shoes. Bring the shoes you plan to wear on your wedding day, including any shoe inserts or lifts you typically use.

📸

Your Inspiration

Photos, Pinterest boards, fabric samples. Anything that communicates what you’re imagining. For custom gown consultations, the more reference material you bring, the more productive the session will be.

How to Prepare for Your Consultation

You don't need to arrive with everything figured out. But the more context you can bring, the more productive your first appointment will be. Here's what helps:

  • Your wedding date (or anticipated date range)
  • The gown itself, if alterations are the goal. And the shoes you plan to wear
  • Photos of looks you love, even if they're from completely different gowns
  • A sense of your undergarments. Strapless bra, shapewear, corset. Since this affects fit
  • Any specific concerns: a bustline that doesn't sit right, a waist that gaps, straps that won't stay
  • An open mind. We often see solutions brides haven't considered

We serve brides from across the Dallas–Fort Worth area, including Plano, Frisco, Allen, McKinney, Southlake, and Fort Worth. Most brides tell us the drive was more than worth it. We'd like to show you why.

What to Expect

Your Consultation,
Step by Step

Step 1. You Arrive with Your Gown

Bring your gown, your wedding shoes, and any undergarments or shapewear you plan to wear on the day. If you have design inspiration photos, bring those too. We work best with a clear visual reference for what you’re imagining. Even rough screenshots from Pinterest or Instagram help us understand your vision faster than description alone.

Step 2. We Assess Your Gown Together

You put the gown on, and Margo West evaluates it with you in the mirror. We look at fit across the bodice, waist, and hips. We assess the hem length with your shoes. We discuss what is and isn’t working, and we talk through every change you want to make. Both structural alterations and any design additions. Nothing is committed to yet; this is a conversation.

Step 3. We Provide a Detailed Quote

After the assessment, we provide a quote for the full scope of work. The quote covers all alterations and design additions discussed. There are no surprise fees added later. If the scope changes at a subsequent fitting. Because you decide to add a bustle, or the fit requires more work than initially visible. We discuss and agree before proceeding. Transparency is the standard here.

Step 4. We Schedule Your Fittings

Once you approve the quote, we schedule your fitting appointments. Most brides need between two and four fittings. For complex alterations or design additions, the fitting schedule may extend over three to five months. We build your timeline around your wedding date and give you a clear picture of when each appointment will happen.
