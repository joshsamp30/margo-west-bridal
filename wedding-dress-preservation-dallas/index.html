How It Works

The Preservation Process

Every gown receives the same careful, methodical approach — from inspection to archival boxing.

01

Drop-Off & Inspection

Your gown is inspected thoroughly. Every stain, fabric type, and embellishment is catalogued. Any needed repairs are noted. You receive a full condition report and a confirmed price.

02

Expert Cleaning

Cleaning is tailored to your specific fabrics — silk, lace, tulle, beading, embroidery. We don’t use a one-size-fits-all process. Stains are pre-treated by hand before any cleaning begins.

03

Pressing & Shaping

Once clean, your gown is carefully pressed and shaped — returning it to its original form. Ruffles re-set, skirts fluffed, bodice structured. The gown is prepared exactly as it was when you wore it.

04

Archival Boxing

Your gown is packed in acid-free tissue and placed in an archival preservation box. Materials are pH-neutral to prevent yellowing for decades. Ready for display, storage, or heirloom transfer.

Why Preserve

What Happens If
You Don’t Preserve

Wedding gowns face three invisible enemies after the wedding: oxidation, humidity, and time. The stains you can’t see today — champagne, cake, sweat — will oxidize and turn yellow or brown within 1–3 years if untreated. White and ivory silks yellow. Lace weakens. Beading loses adhesion. Embroidery threads break.

Preservation stops all of it. The sooner after the wedding, the better — but we regularly work with gowns stored for years. It’s never too late to protect what matters.

  • ✦ Prevents yellowing and oxidation of white & ivory fabrics
  • ✦ Removes invisible sugar stains before they set permanently
  • ✦ Protects beading, lace, and embellishment integrity
  • ✦ Archival boxing prevents humidity and pest damage
  • ✦ Creates an heirloom for daughters, granddaughters, & beyond
