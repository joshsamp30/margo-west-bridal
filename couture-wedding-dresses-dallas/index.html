The Couture Journey

Four Steps to
Your Singular Gown

01
Design Consultation

We start with you — your aesthetic, your wedding vision, your must-haves and boundaries. Margo works with you to develop a silhouette, structural approach, and embellishment direction before a single fabric is cut.

02
Fabric & Material Selection

From silk charmeuse and duchess satin to delicate Chantilly lace and hand-beaded appliqué — we source materials to match your vision and construction requirements exactly. You'll see, touch, and approve everything.

03
Construction & Fittings

Your gown is built in stages, with private fittings scheduled to shape every seam directly on your body. This is where couture becomes real — adjustments made in the moment, fit refined over multiple sessions, until the silhouette is exactly right.

04
Final Fitting & Delivery

The final fitting happens 2–4 weeks before your wedding. Any last adjustments are made, the gown is finished and pressed, and we walk you through everything you need to know about wearing and carrying your gown on the day.

About Couture Wedding Gowns

The terms are often used interchangeably, but there is a meaningful distinction. Custom often refers to a made-to-order gown — an existing design reproduced in your measurements. Couture means the gown is designed and constructed from scratch specifically for you. At Margo West, our couture work begins at the design stage: no existing pattern is used, and the gown takes shape through a series of fittings on your body specifically.

A couture wedding dress is a one-of-a-kind gown designed and built entirely to the individual bride — to her exact measurements, personal aesthetic, and structural needs. It involves a series of private fittings where the gown is shaped directly on the wearer, hand-finished details, and materials selected specifically for that gown. The result exists nowhere else in the world.

Couture wedding dress pricing reflects the labor, materials, and time involved in creating a singular piece. At Margo West, we provide a full project quote after your initial design consultation — pricing is based on your specific silhouette, fabric selection, embellishment detail, and construction complexity. We begin with a $100 consultation deposit applied to your total. Schedule a consultation to discuss your vision and receive a quote.

Most couture wedding gowns require 4–8 months from design consultation to final fitting, depending on complexity and embellishment level. We recommend beginning at least 6 months before your wedding.
