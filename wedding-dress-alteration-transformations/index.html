The Art of Transformation

We Do the Work
Others Turn Away

Most bridal shops do alterations. Very few do transformations. At Margo West Bridal Couture in Dallas’s Design District, we specialize in the dramatic changes that other seamstresses and bridal boutiques decline to attempt.

We’ve added hand-crafted illusion sleeves to strapless gowns. We’ve rebuilt entire bodices from scratch. We’ve converted ball gowns into sleek fitted silhouettes and added cathedral trains to simple sheaths. Every piece of this work demands couture-level skill, patience, and the 40+ years of expertise that Margo West brings to each and every appointment.

“I won’t do something halfway. When a bride comes to me with a vision, I either commit fully to making it happen — or I tell her honestly that she should seek it elsewhere. We almost always make it happen.”
— Margo West, Founder

What We Transform

Six Categories of Couture Transformation

From structural rebuilds to delicate handwork — each transformation type requires a different mastery of craft. We do them all.

01

Silhouette Changes

Converting ball gowns to A-lines, sheaths to mermaids, or restructuring any silhouette to flatter your exact proportions. These are complex structural alterations requiring full deconstruction and rebuilding from the foundation.

Structural
02

Sleeve Additions

Creating illusion sleeves, lace cap sleeves, long fitted sleeves, or detachable sleeves from scratch to match existing gown fabric, lace, or beading. A specialty of Margo West that draws brides from across North Texas.

Specialty
03

Train Additions & Changes

Adding cathedral, chapel, or royal trains to gowns that had none. Converting train styles. Designing custom detachable overskirts. Creating sweep trains from gown fabric for a seamless, intentional look.

Construction
04

Bodice Redesigns

Replacing necklines, converting sweetheart to illusion, raising or lowering backs, adding or removing boning, converting strapless to strapped. Complete bodice rebuilds when the gown’s top portion needs full reimagining.

Redesign
05

Beading & Embellishment

Adding beading, sequins, or appliqués to a plain gown. Repairing damaged lace or embroidery. Extending beadwork patterns across added sleeves or a new back panel. Painstaking handwork that transforms a simple gown into something extraordinary.

Handwork
06

Heirloom & Vintage Restoration

Modernizing a mother’s or grandmother’s gown for a contemporary bride. Repairing fragile vintage lace and fabric. Updating construction while honoring the original design. Heritage preserved, silhouette renewed.

Heirloom

How It Works

Your Transformation Journey

Every dramatic alteration begins with an honest conversation about what’s possible. Here’s how we turn your vision into reality.

01

Consultation & Vision

A $100 consultation deposit secures Margo’s undivided attention. You share your vision, we evaluate your gown, and we have an honest conversation about what’s achievable.

02

Design & Planning

We sketch the transformation, source matching materials, and develop a detailed plan with clear milestones, timeline, and total cost estimate.

03

Couture Craftsmanship

The transformation begins. Whether it takes 20 hours or 120 hours, every detail receives the same exacting couture standard — no shortcuts, no compromise.

04

Fitting & Refinement

Multiple fittings ensure the transformation fits your body perfectly. We adjust, refine, and perfect until you can’t imagine your gown any other way.
