What We Alter

Every Type of Wedding Dress Alteration

From a simple hem to a complete structural transformation — if it can be done with couture-level craftsmanship, we do it.

✂️

Bodice & Fit Alterations

Taking in or letting out at the bodice, waist, and hips. Reshaping seams for a precise, custom fit. Adjusting boning and structure for all-day comfort without sacrificing silhouette.

👗

Hemming & Bustle Work

Cathedral, chapel, and floor-length hemming with invisible finishes. French bustles, American bustles, and custom bustle designs that move beautifully all night long.

🪡

Sleeve Additions & Removal

Adding illusion sleeves, detachable lace sleeves, cap sleeves, or full sleeves to any gown. Removing sleeves to create a new silhouette. Built from scratch to perfectly match your gown’s fabric and lace.

💎

Beading & Lace Work

Matching and extending lace patterns, adding beaded appliqués, hand-sewing crystal detailing, and replicating existing embellishments across new fabric panels. Couture-level precision only.

🌿

Train Additions & Modifications

Adding a cathedral or chapel train to a gown that didn’t come with one. Converting a full train to a detachable style for the reception. Altering the shape or length of an existing train.

✦

Structural Redesigns

Complete silhouette transformations — converting a ballgown to a fit-and-flare, adding a second look reception skirt, restructuring a strapless bodice, or dramatically changing the back design. The work other designers won’t attempt.

The Process

From First Fitting to Final Touch

Every bridal alteration at Margo West follows a careful, collaborative process built around you — not a checklist.

1

Consultation

$100 deposit. Margo assesses your gown, discusses your vision, and outlines exactly what’s possible. Quote provided at this appointment.

2

First Fitting

Measurements taken. Pinning and marking the exact alterations. Bring your wedding shoes and any shapewear you plan to wear.

3

Couture Work

The artistry happens here. Every stitch placed with precision. Complex work like sleeves, beading, or structural changes handled by Margo personally.

4

Second Fitting

You try the gown on the altered version. Refinements made. Any last adjustments confirmed and completed.

5

Final Pickup

Your gown is pressed, protected, and ready. Final fitting 2–4 weeks before your wedding date — everything perfect.

We recommend scheduling your first consultation 4–6 months before your wedding date. Rush timelines available — call (972) 918-9750 to discuss.
